# MusicMood Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-06-13_

MusicMood is an Android application designed to help you understand the relationship between your music listening habits and your emotional well-being. The app operates on a principle of automated, passive analysis combined with user-controlled privacy.

- **Music Detection & Analysis:** The app observes the songs you listen to natively on your device. Rather than relying on simple, generic database tags, basic track details are sent to Google's Gemini AI service for analysis — extracting multi-dimensional musicological qualities (such as musical intensity, vocal delivery, tempo ranges, and core lyrical themes) that populate your personal on-device baseline.

- **Contextual Integration & Summarization:** To transform raw listening metrics into meaningful insights, MusicMood correlates your song history with real-time context. This includes your location categories (like home, work, or gym), calendar events, weather, time of day, and current physical activity (such as driving, walking, or running), alongside physiological metrics (like heart rate and HRV-derived stress) from your wearable devices. To compile comprehensive daily summaries, anonymous contextual baselines and raw biometric range data — stripped of identity markers — are processed by Google's Gemini AI service to generate the natural-language summary you see in-app.

- **Personalized Baseline:** By combining this data with specific details you provide about what different types of music mean to you, the app establishes a personalized listener profile unique to your habits.

- **Longitudinal Trends:** Your data is structured into distinct listening sessions that aggregate into days, weeks, and months, allowing you to see how your habits shift over time.

- **Predictive Mood Journaling:** This continuous history enables a smart mood-journaling interface. Instead of forcing you to face a blank page, the app reduces the effort of journaling by providing a predicted mood for the day alongside contextual examples of what you did and listened to, allowing you to easily validate, adjust, or reflect on your emotional trends.

The purpose of this document is to provide complete transparency regarding the specific device permissions MusicMood requests and to explain exactly how they are used to power your experience.

### Stored on your device

- **Listening history** — for each song played: title, artist, album, the time it was played, how long you listened, whether you skipped, whether you paused.
- **Per-song context, captured at the moment a song plays:**
  - Activity (still / walking / driving) from Android's Activity Recognition API.
  - Coarse location category (home / work / school / commuting / elsewhere) — derived from your GPS coordinates compared against the locations you configured in the app.
  - Current weather (temperature, conditions), fetched by GPS coordinates.
  - Whether you're in a calendar event, and the event's category (work meeting, doctor's appointment, etc.), if you've granted calendar permission.
  - Heart rate, stress (derived from HRV), and recent sleep data, if you've granted Health Connect permission.
- **Journal entries** — free-text mood reflections that you write.
- **AI Song Analyses** — Structured musicological profiles returned by Google Gemini (including genre, vocal delivery style, tempo ranges, affective mood, and core lyrical themes) are saved locally to your history database.
- **Daily insights and mood summaries** — text generated by Google Gemini from your recent listening + context data, plus journal entries you choose to share with the analysis.
- **Album art** — image files cached from the playing app's media notification.
- **Diagnostic logs** — plain-text event log saved under `Documents/MusicMood/logs/`, used to debug issues.

All of the above is stored in an on-device SQLite database (`musicmood.db`), in cached image files, and in text log files. None of it is uploaded to any server we control — MusicMood does not currently operate any server infrastructure of its own.

## Data transmitted off your device

These services receive the listed inputs only — no other data, no account identifiers, and no historical behavioral timelines beyond the current request.

| Service | What we send | What it returns |
|---|---|---|
| **Google Gemini API** <br>(Google LLC) | Song title, artist, album, optional release metadata (release year, genre). For session mood summaries: anonymized session-level context including aggregated activity, location category, weather, time of day, and biometric ranges (e.g. stress range from HRV) — never raw GPS coordinates, never event titles, never raw heart rate samples. Plus the contents of journal entries you choose to include in the analysis. | Per-song musicological analysis (genre, mood, vocal style, lyrical themes, confidence) and session-level natural-language mood summaries. |
| **MusicBrainz** <br>(MetaBrainz Foundation, public web service) | Song title, artist, optional album | Release year, album name, track duration |
| **Last.fm** <br>(Audioscrobbler / CBS Interactive) | Song title, artist | Listener count and community tags. Used as a popularity anchor for AI confidence calibration; never queries your personal listening history. Only active when a Last.fm API key is configured in the build. |
| **LrcLib** <br>(lrclib.net) | Song title, artist, optional album and duration | Synchronized lyrics |
| **Genius** <br>(Genius Media Group) | Song title, artist (only if LrcLib fails) | Lyrics |
| **Open-Meteo** <br>(open-meteo.com) | Latitude/longitude | Current weather conditions |
| **Overpass / OpenStreetMap** <br>(overpass-api.de) | Latitude/longitude | Local road speed limit (used to detect slow traffic when driving) |

Use of each third-party service is subject to that service's privacy policy. None of these services receive your name, email, permanent device identifier, full historical timelines, unshared journal entries, calendar contents, or raw biometric samples. Requests sent to Google Gemini are subject to Google's Generative AI API terms.

## Data retention and deletion

- All on-device history and cache data is retained for as long as you keep the app installed.
- Uninstalling the app removes all of the above through Android's normal app-data cleanup.
- The "Export Data" option in Settings exports a copy of the SQLite database to your Documents/MusicMood folder.
- The "Clear All Data" option in Settings deletes the database and all cached files.
- Individual entries (a single song play, a journal entry, an entire session) can be deleted from the relevant screen in the app.

## Sharing with third parties

We do not sell, rent, or share your data with any third party for advertising, analytics, or marketing purposes.

The third-party APIs listed above receive only the inputs specified. They are not provided with your listening history, journal entries, health metrics, or any identifier tying their request back to you.

## NOT collected and NOT sent anywhere

- Audio recordings (the app reads metadata from media notifications; it does not record audio).
- Your name, email address, phone number, or any account identifier.
- Device identifiers (Advertising ID, IMEI, etc.).
- Calendar event contents — calendar permission is used to detect whether you're in an event of a given category (work, medical, etc.); the event title and details stay on your device.
- Health metrics (heart rate, sleep) beyond the anonymized aggregated ranges described above — raw samples and any identifiable clinical profile never leave your phone.
- Coarse-or-finer location beyond what's needed for weather and road speed lookups; the lat/lng we send to those specific APIs is the only location data that leaves your device.

## Children's privacy

MusicMood is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you are a parent and believe your child has been using MusicMood, uninstalling the app removes any locally-stored data.

## Changes to this policy

If MusicMood's data handling changes, this policy will be updated and the "Last updated" date above will change. Material changes that affect what data is collected or shared will also be disclosed in-app.
